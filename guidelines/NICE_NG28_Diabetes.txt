# NICE Guideline NG28: Type 2 Diabetes in Adults - Management
## Sample Clinical Guideline Content for Testing

### Overview
This guideline covers the care and management of type 2 diabetes in adults (aged 18 and over). It includes advice on diet, lifestyle, education, managing blood glucose levels, cardiovascular risk, and identifying and managing long-term complications.

### Key Recommendations

#### 1. Individualised Care
- Adopt an individualised approach to diabetes care tailored to:
  * Clinical and personal circumstances
  * Personal preferences and needs
  * Comorbidities
  * Risks from polypharmacy
  * Life expectancy
- Involve people with diabetes in decisions about their care
- Provide opportunities for diabetes education

#### 2. HbA1c Targets and Monitoring
- Aim for HbA1c target of 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) for adults managed with lifestyle and diet, or lifestyle and a single drug not associated with hypoglycaemia
- Target of 53 mmol/mol (7.0%) for adults on drugs associated with hypoglycaemia
- In adults with type 2 diabetes, measure HbA1c levels every 3–6 months until stable, then every 6 months
- Consider relaxing HbA1c targets in:
  * Frail elderly patients
  * Those with reduced life expectancy
  * Those prone to hypoglycaemia

#### 3. Blood Glucose Management - First-Line Therapy

##### Lifestyle Interventions
- Offer structured education at diagnosis and annually thereafter
- Dietary advice:
  * High-fibre, low-glycaemic-index carbohydrates
  * Low-fat dairy products
  * Oily fish
  * Control portion sizes for weight management
- Physical activity:
  * 150 minutes moderate-intensity activity per week
  * Resistance training on 2+ days per week

##### Metformin as First-Line
- Offer standard-release metformin as initial drug treatment
- Starting dose: 500mg once daily with breakfast
- Titrate up gradually over several weeks to maintenance dose
- Usual maintenance: 1000mg twice daily
- Maximum dose: 1000mg three times daily or 2000mg once daily (modified release)
- If gastro-intestinal side effects develop, consider modified-release metformin

#### 4. Dual Therapy (If HbA1c ≥58 mmol/mol / 7.5%)
Consider adding one of the following to metformin:
- **DPP-4 inhibitor** (e.g., sitagliptin, linagliptin)
  * If hypoglycaemia is significant risk
  * Weight gain would cause problems
- **Pioglitazone**
  * Only if DPP-4 inhibitor would be contraindicated
  * Monitor for heart failure, bladder cancer
- **Sulfonylurea** (e.g., gliclazide, glimepiride)
  * If rapid glucose reduction needed
  * Accept hypoglycaemia risk
  * Start at lowest dose, titrate up
- **SGLT2 inhibitor** (e.g., dapagliflozin, empagliflozin)
  * Particularly if CKD or heart failure
  * Monitor for genital infections

#### 5. Triple Therapy (If HbA1c ≥58 mmol/mol / 7.5%)
Consider metformin + two of:
- DPP-4 inhibitor
- Pioglitazone
- Sulfonylurea
- SGLT2 inhibitor

Or consider insulin-based treatment

#### 6. Insulin Therapy
Indications:
- HbA1c ≥58 mmol/mol on triple therapy
- Symptomatic hyperglycaemia
- Metabolic decompensation

Regimens:
- **NPH insulin** (isophane insulin) at bedtime or twice daily
- **Pre-mixed insulin** twice daily
- Continue metformin, consider stopping other oral drugs
- Titrate insulin dose based on self-monitoring

#### 7. GLP-1 Mimetics
Consider if:
- Triple therapy not effective or not tolerated
- BMI ≥35 kg/m² (or ≥32.5 kg/m² in South Asian/Chinese)
- Weight-related comorbidities
- Insulin would have occupational implications

Continue only if:
- HbA1c reduction ≥11 mmol/mol (1%)
- Weight loss ≥3% at 6 months

#### 8. Cardiovascular Risk Management

##### Blood Pressure
- Target BP <140/90 mmHg (or <130/80 mmHg if kidney, eye, or cerebrovascular damage)
- Offer ACE inhibitor (or ARB if ACE-I not tolerated) as first-line
- Add calcium channel blocker or thiazide-like diuretic as second-line

##### Lipid Management
- Offer atorvastatin 20mg for primary prevention if QRISK2 ≥10%
- For secondary prevention, offer atorvastatin 80mg
- Aim for non-HDL reduction >40% from baseline

##### Antiplatelet Therapy
- Offer aspirin 75mg for secondary prevention
- Do not offer for primary prevention routinely

#### 9. Monitoring for Complications

##### Annual Reviews
- HbA1c and glucose control
- Blood pressure
- Lipid profile
- Kidney function (eGFR, ACR)
- Foot examination
- Eye screening (retinal photography)
- Weight and BMI
- Smoking status
- Alcohol consumption

##### Diabetic Kidney Disease
- Measure urinary albumin:creatinine ratio (ACR) annually
- If ACR ≥3 mg/mmol:
  * Offer ACE-I or ARB
  * Target BP <130/80 mmHg
  * Consider SGLT2 inhibitor

##### Diabetic Retinopathy
- Annual digital retinal photography screening
- Refer urgently if:
  * Preproliferative or proliferative retinopathy
  * Maculopathy
  * Visual symptoms

##### Diabetic Neuropathy
- Assess for peripheral neuropathy annually
- Examine feet for ulcers, calluses, deformities
- Assess vascular status (pulses, capillary refill)
- Risk stratify and provide appropriate foot care education

#### 10. Managing Hypoglycaemia
- Educate about recognition and treatment
- For conscious patient: 15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tablets, juice)
- Re-check glucose after 15 minutes
- If unconscious: IM glucagon or IV glucose
- Review medication causing hypoglycaemia
- Consider continuous glucose monitoring if recurrent

#### 11. Sick Day Rules
Advise patients to:
- Continue usual diabetes medications during illness
- Exception: stop metformin if risk of dehydration or AKI
- Temporarily stop SGLT2 inhibitors during acute illness
- Monitor blood glucose more frequently
- Maintain hydration
- Seek medical advice if:
  * Unable to eat/drink
  * Persistent vomiting
  * Blood glucose >15 mmol/L
  * Ketones present

#### 12. Special Situations

##### Ramadan Fasting
- Pre-Ramadan assessment of risk
- Adjust medication to reduce hypoglycaemia risk
- Consider switching sulfonylurea to DPP-4 inhibitor
- Educate about breaking fast if unwell

##### Surgery
- Aim for glucose 6-10 mmol/L perioperatively
- Variable rate IV insulin for major surgery
- Continue long-acting insulin at 80% normal dose
- Restart usual therapy when eating/drinking

##### Pregnancy Planning
- Aim for HbA1c <48 mmol/mol before conception
- Stop oral agents except metformin
- Offer retinal assessment
- High-dose folic acid (5mg daily)

### Patient Education Components
- Nature and progression of type 2 diabetes
- Lifestyle modification strategies
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose
- Medication adherence and timing
- Hypoglycaemia prevention and management
- Sick day rules
- Complication screening importance
- Foot care
- When to seek medical help

### Quality Metrics
1. HbA1c ≤58 mmol/mol in 60% of patients
2. Blood pressure <140/80 mmHg in 70% of patients
3. Total cholesterol <5 mmol/L in 70% of patients
4. 100% completion of annual review elements
5. 90% uptake of structured education within 12 months of diagnosis

### Audit Standards
- Documentation of individualized HbA1c targets
- Timely treatment intensification when targets not met
- Annual monitoring of complications
- Patient education offered and documented
- Cardiovascular risk assessment completed
